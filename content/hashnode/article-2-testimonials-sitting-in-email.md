You know that email?

The one where a customer says: *"Just wanted to say — this completely changed how I run my business. I recommend you to everyone."*

You read it, felt great, maybe replied with a thank you. Then you filed it under... somewhere. And it never made it to your website.

You're not alone. This is one of the most common missed opportunities in small business marketing.

## Why testimonials die in your inbox

**Friction.** Getting a testimonial from email to your website isn't one step — it's five:

1. Copy the text out of email
2. Decide where on your site it should go
3. Log into your CMS or ask your developer
4. Format it (who said it? their title? their photo?)
5. Actually push the change live

By step 2, something else has already pulled your attention. The testimonial stays in your inbox. The cycle repeats.

**No system.** Most small business owners don't have a testimonial collection process — they get them reactively, when customers volunteer them. There's no collection link to share, no submission form, no place reviews land that isn't someone's personal inbox.

**No ownership.** If reviews live in your email, they're invisible. Google doesn't see them. Visitors don't see them. The people who most need convincing — potential customers comparing you to competitors — never see them.

## Why this actually matters

Here's the uncomfortable truth about small business websites: most visitors are skeptical. They've been burned before. They want proof that you deliver what you promise — not from you, but from someone like them.

Testimonials are that proof. A single specific, authentic testimonial ("She redesigned my whole brand in 2 weeks and my bookings doubled") does more conversion work than any headline you could write.

But it only works if it's *on your site*.

## What a working system looks like

The simplest possible version:

1. **A collection link** — a URL you can send to happy customers that takes them straight to a short form. "Mind leaving a quick testimonial? It takes 2 minutes: [link]"
2. **An approval step** — you review what comes in before anything goes public
3. **A widget** — a snippet of code you drop into your website once, that pulls in approved testimonials automatically

That's it. No CSV exports. No developer tickets. No three-step CMS update every time someone says something nice about you.
