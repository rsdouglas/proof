## Body (plain prose, 200 words)

```
Hi HN — I'm rsdouglas and this is SocialProof.dev (https://socialproof.dev).

The short version: you get a link, you send it to customers, they leave a testimonial, and it shows up on your site via one script tag. The whole thing is free for one widget.

The longer version: I'm building this entirely with AI agents. The marketing agent writes copy and files PRs. The ops agent monitors the build. There's no human writing the blog posts or drafting the community outreach — it's all autonomous. I'm the only human, and my job is mostly reviewing what the agents ship and occasionally unblocking them when they hit a wall they can't climb.

It's early. The product works end-to-end — you can actually collect testimonials and embed them — but activated users are near zero because distribution is hard when you're not the one doing the posting.

I built it because small businesses are terrible at collecting testimonials even when their customers love them. The friction is always "how do I ask?" and "where does it go?" This is the answer.

Would genuinely appreciate feedback on the product, the positioning, and honestly the AI-built-in-public experiment itself.

https://socialproof.dev
```

## Alternative Body (if rsdouglas prefers product-only, no AI meta-story)

```
Hi HN — I'm rsdouglas and this is SocialProof.dev (https://socialproof.dev).

The problem: small businesses have happy customers but almost none of them have testimonials on their websites. The ask is awkward, the setup feels technical, and it keeps falling to the bottom of the to-do list.

What I built: a shareable link that takes customers straight to a testimonial form. No login required on their end, no Google account, no third-party review site. You get a dashboard to approve submissions, and a one-line script tag to show them on your site. That's the whole thing.

It works on any platform — WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, Shopify, or plain HTML. The widget updates automatically when you approve new testimonials, so you add the script once and forget it.

It's early — the core flow works end-to-end but I'm still figuring out whether "shareable link" is the right mental model or whether I should go more like a hosted review page. Would appreciate any feedback on the UX and positioning.

https://socialproof.dev
```
