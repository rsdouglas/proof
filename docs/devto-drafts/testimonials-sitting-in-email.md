## The problem isn't that you don't have testimonials

It's that the collection-to-display pipeline is broken.

The traditional flow looks like this:

1. Customer says something nice (email, text, DM, in person)
2. You think "I should put that on my website"
3. You forget, or you don't know how
4. Six months later: you still don't have testimonials on your site

The bottleneck isn't the customers — it's the friction between "customer says nice thing" and "nice thing appears on website."

---

## Why testimonials matter so much for small businesses specifically

For big brands, trust comes from recognition. Everyone knows Nike. You don't need testimonials to trust that your shoes will arrive.

For small businesses, trust comes from other customers. Before someone books a massage therapist they found on Google, they want to know: have other people tried this? Were they happy? Is this person legitimate?

A single testimonial from a real customer saying "Best massage I've had in 10 years, I've already booked my next appointment" does more work than any headline you write about yourself.

The research backs this up. Nielsen finds that 92% of consumers trust peer recommendations over brand advertising. BrightLocal found that 88% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations.

Your testimonials are your most powerful marketing asset. They're just not deployed yet.

This is the key shift: instead of collecting testimonials reactively (waiting for nice emails), you collect them proactively (sending a link right after a good experience).

### Step 2: Ask at the right moment (takes 30 seconds)

The best time to ask for a testimonial is immediately after a positive experience — before the customer moves on to other things.

For service businesses: right when you wrap up a job.
For coaches and consultants: when a client hits a milestone or completes a program.
For product businesses: a few days after delivery, when they've had time to use it.

The ask can be casual:

> "Hey [name], really glad you're happy with [service/product]. Would you be willing to leave a quick testimonial? It takes about a minute: [your link]"

That's it. No fancy ask required.
