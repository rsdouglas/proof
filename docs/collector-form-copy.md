# Collector Form — Copy Spec

This document defines the copy for the public-facing testimonial collection form.
This is what a business owner's *customers* see when they're asked to leave a review.

## Philosophy

The form should feel like doing something **kind** for a business you liked — not filling out a survey.
Every word should lower friction and increase warmth.

---

## Form Copy

### Page title
```
How was your experience with {business_name}?
```

### Subheading
```
Your honest words help others find them — and mean the world to a small business.
```

### Star rating field
- Label: `Your rating`
- (Interactive stars — 1 to 5)

### Name field
- Label: `Your name`
- Placeholder: `Jane Smith`
- Helper text: `Shown publicly with your testimonial`

### Testimonial text field
- Label: `Your experience`
- Placeholder: `What was it like working with them? What would you tell a friend who was considering them?`
- Min length: 20 characters
- Max length: 500 characters

### Photo upload (optional)
- Label: `Add a photo (optional)`
- Helper: `A headshot makes your testimonial 3× more credible`

### Submit button
```
Share my experience →
```

### Success screen
**Headline:**
```
Thank you, {first_name}!
```

**Body:**
```
{business_name} will review your testimonial shortly.
Your words make a real difference for a small business.
```

---

## Tone notes

- Never say "submit" — use "share" or "send"
- Never say "form" — it's a conversation, not paperwork
- Always use the business name — personalization increases completion
- Keep the whole form on one screen if possible — each scroll kills conversions

## A/B test ideas

| Variant | Subheading | Expected effect |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| A (control) | "Your honest words help others find them" | Baseline |
| B | "This takes 60 seconds and helps a small business grow" | Time + impact framing |
| C | "Be their first testimonial — it matters most" | Scarcity for new accounts |
