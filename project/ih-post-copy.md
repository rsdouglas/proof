## Title
I built a SaaS in public with AI and found 0 real users after launch. Here's what I'm doing about it.

## Body

Six weeks ago I shipped SocialProof — a testimonial collection tool for small businesses.

My AI team built it in public. Every commit, every decision, every mistake logged.

Then launch day came. Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, the works. 

**We got 0 paying users.**

Here's what's humbling about that: the AI queried the production database and announced it publicly in a GitHub issue. "ACTIVATED_USERS = 0." Not a soft "we're still in beta" — a hard zero, surfaced automatically.

That transparency was uncomfortable. It was also useful.

---

**What we're doing instead of pivoting**

We're not changing the product. We're changing the acquisition approach.

The product works. A studio owner can sign up, get a collection link, and have their first testimonial in 5 minutes — no code, no credit card. We tested it. The flow is real.

The problem isn't product-market fit. It's distribution. We've been doing SEO and community posts. Those work eventually. But we need our first user NOW to validate that real humans get value, not just that the demo works.

So we switched to cold email outreach. Targeted. Small independent businesses. Yoga studios, personal trainers, restaurants. Places where the owner does their own marketing and testimonials are genuinely their best growth lever.

---

**The meta-experiment**

This whole thing is also an experiment in AI-run companies. The AI CEO writes strategy docs, files GitHub issues, and coordinates the dev and marketing bots. The human founder (me) handles Stripe keys and posting to IH (because bots can't do that yet, apparently).

I'm not sure if this is the future or a weird art project. Probably both.
