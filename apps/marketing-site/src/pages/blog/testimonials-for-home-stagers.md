# Testimonials for Home Stagers: How Reviews Help You Win More Listings

Home staging is a referral business. Most home stagers get the majority of their work through real estate agents who've seen results firsthand. But what happens when an agent passes along your name to a seller, or when someone finds you through search or Instagram?

That's where testimonials do the work. They're the proof that converts a referral into a booking.

## Why Testimonials Matter for Home Stagers

When a seller is interviewing two or three home stagers, they're choosing based on portfolio and trust. Your portfolio shows your aesthetic. Your testimonials show your reliability.

The questions testimonials answer:
- "Will the house actually sell faster and for more after staging?"
- "Is this person easy to work with under deadline pressure?"
- "Will they respect my furniture and space?"

A testimonial from a real estate agent carries particular weight: it signals that professionals in the industry trust you. A testimonial from a homeowner who sold above asking price is a direct pitch to future sellers.

## What Great Home Stager Testimonials Look Like

**From a real estate agent:**
> "I've worked with three stagers over my career and Maria is the one I call for every listing now. She turned a dated 1970s split-level into something buyers wanted to compete for. We listed at $465K and got $492K after 6 days. She's always on time, always under budget, and never needs handholding."

**From a homeowner:**
> "I was skeptical staging would make a real difference. We accepted an offer $28K over asking after just 4 days on the market. Worth every penny. Also, everything was back to normal the day after closing — she made it completely stress-free."

Notice what these do: specific outcomes (days on market, sale price over asking), testimonials about process (on time, easy to work with), and a clear signal to the next client.

## Who to Ask for Testimonials

**Real estate agents** — your most valuable source. They see staging results across dozens of listings. One strong agent quote can carry your business. After each listing sells, send a quick email: "I saw [address] sold above asking! Would you be willing to leave a quick testimonial for my site?"

**Homeowners** — more emotional, but highly relatable to future sellers. Reach out within 2 weeks of closing while the experience is fresh.

**Developers and investors** — if you stage model units or flip properties, these clients understand ROI directly. Their testimonials appeal to other investor clients.

## Asking for the Right Detail

Generic testimonials don't close deals. Prompt clients with specific questions:

- "How many days was the property on the market?"
- "Did it sell above asking?"
- "What feedback did buyers or agents give about the presentation?"

You can offer to draft the testimonial for them based on these answers — busy agents especially appreciate this. They review and approve; you get a quote that actually converts.

## Where to Display Testimonials

**Your website** — add a testimonials section to your homepage and services page. Include a mix of agent and homeowner quotes.

**Google Business Profile** — reviews here appear in local search results when people search "home stager [city]." Ask clients to also post their testimonial as a Google review.

**Instagram** — screenshot strong quotes and share them as posts or Stories. Agents and sellers follow local staging accounts; social proof here builds your audience.

**Real estate marketing materials** — if you co-market with an agent (flyers, listing pages), adding your testimonials builds your brand with their seller clients.
