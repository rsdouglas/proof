When a parent is looking for a driving school, the stakes feel high. They're handing their teenager's safety to a stranger. Reviews from other parents who felt the same way — and were impressed — are your most powerful marketing tool.

## Who Is Searching

- Parents of 15-16 year olds beginning driver education
- Teens who failed their road test and need more practice
- Adult new drivers (immigrants, people who grew up in cities, late starters)
- Nervous adult drivers returning after a long break or accident

Each segment has different anxieties. Segment your testimonials accordingly.

## When to Ask

**After passing the road test:** The highest-emotion moment:
> "Congratulations! This is such a big deal. Would you — or your parents — take 2 minutes to share the experience on Google? It helps other families find our program: [link]"

**After completing the behind-the-wheel package:** Even without the test yet, completing the course is a milestone worth celebrating:
> "You've completed all your sessions! Hoping it's been a great experience. If so, we'd love a quick review: [link]"

## What a Strong Driving School Review Looks Like

*"Our daughter was terrified to drive. After 6 lessons with [instructor name], she passed her road test on the first try. The instructor was patient, clear, and never made her feel bad when she made mistakes. We've already told everyone in her friend group. Worth every penny."*

Strong elements: the before-state (terrified), the outcome (passed first try), the instructor quality, and word-of-mouth spillover.
