New mothers searching for lactation support are often in crisis. A baby who won't latch, pain that's making every feed a battle, supply concerns at 2am — these are exhausted, stressed, sometimes tearful people who need help *now*.

When they search "lactation consultant near me," they are going to trust whoever has the reviews that say: *She saved our breastfeeding journey.*

That could be you.

## Who Is Searching

- First-time mothers struggling with latch, supply, or pain
- Mothers of NICU infants managing pumping and transition to breast
- Mothers returning to work who want to maintain supply with pumping
- Mothers with multiples (twins, triplets) navigating feeding complexity
- Mothers who received contradictory advice and need authoritative guidance
- Partners supporting a struggling breastfeeding mother

## When to Ask

**At the follow-up after the first in-home consultation:**
> "I hope things are already feeling more manageable. If our session made a real difference, I'd be so grateful if you'd share that with other moms in the same situation: [link]"

**After the feeding struggle resolves:**
> "You did the work and you got there. Lots of moms are going through what you went through. A review from you would help them find support: [link]"

**After a successful return-to-work pumping plan:**
> "You've been back at work for a month and still going! That takes real commitment. If you'd share your experience, it helps other working moms know it's possible: [link]"

## Privacy Considerations

Like other healthcare-adjacent fields, your clients are sharing vulnerable information. Before asking for a review:
- Keep your request general — don't reference specific clinical details
- Let the mother choose what to share (or not share) in their public review
- For website testimonials, get explicit written consent for any identifying details

## What a Strong Lactation Consultant Review Looks Like

*"By day 5 postpartum, I was ready to quit. Every feed left me in tears. [Name] came to our house the next morning and in two hours completely transformed our latch. She showed me three different positions and explained exactly what was wrong. By that afternoon, I had my first pain-free feed. Three months later, we're still going and I never thought that was possible. If you're struggling — please call her before you give up."*

Strong elements: crisis moment, rapid resolution, specific technique mentioned, emotional before/after, long-term outcome, and urgent endorsement.
