Event photography is a repeat-booking business. Corporate clients who loved working with you will hire you for their next conference, gala, product launch, or team photo day. Individual clients will recommend you to friends for their milestone events.

The key to unlocking that cycle? Testimonials that capture what made working with you exceptional.

## Who Hires Event Photographers (And What They Care About)

**Corporate clients** (marketing managers, event coordinators, HR teams) care about:
- Professionalism and reliability (showing up on time, dressed appropriately)
- Ability to work independently without constant direction
- Fast turnaround for deliverables they need for press releases and social media
- Understanding the corporate aesthetic and brand guidelines

**Individual clients** (milestone parties, quinceañeras, anniversaries, retirement parties) care about:
- Natural, candid shots that feel authentic vs. overly posed
- Capturing key family moments and group dynamics
- Being personable and unobtrusive
- Getting images they'll print and display

Build separate testimonial sets for each audience.

## The Best Time to Ask: When You Deliver the Photos

The moment a client receives their gallery and opens it for the first time is peak satisfaction. That's your ask window.

**With gallery delivery email:**
> "Your gallery is ready! [link to gallery]
>
> We had a wonderful time at [event]. If you're happy with the photos, a quick review would mean so much — it helps other event planners and clients find us: [link to review page]
>
> Looking forward to working with you again at [future event / next year's conference]!"

## Scripts for Corporate vs. Individual

**Corporate (marketing manager / event planner):**
> "Hi [Name], the gallery from [Conference/Event] is ready. We hope it captures the energy and professionalism of the event perfectly. If you're happy with the results, a quick review from your team's perspective would be hugely helpful for other event coordinators who are evaluating photographers: [link]"

**Individual client:**
> "Hi [Name], your [Anniversary / Birthday / Party] gallery is ready — [link]. We loved capturing your celebration! If you have a moment to share a few words about your experience, it would mean so much to our small business: [link]"

## What Makes a Great Event Photography Testimonial

**Corporate:**
*"We've hired [Photographer] for our annual conference three years running. She knows our brand standards, works independently without micromanaging, and always delivers a full gallery within 48 hours — exactly what we need for our post-event social and PR. She's on our preferred vendor list for good reason."*

**Individual:**
*"I wanted candid shots of my parents' 50th anniversary party, not stiff posed photos. [Photographer] disappeared into the crowd and caught moments I didn't even realize happened — my dad tearing up during the toast, my kids dancing with their grandparents. These are the photos we'll have forever."*

Strong testimonials mention:
- Specific event type
- Professionalism and independence
- Turnaround time
- Specific memorable shots or moments
- Intent to rebook

## The Corporate Repeat Client Testimonial

Corporate clients who've booked you multiple times are your most valuable testimonial source. They speak to reliability over time:

> "Hi [Name], you've now trusted us with [X] events — that means a lot to us. Would you be willing to share your experience in a review? It specifically helps other corporate event planners who are evaluating photographers for the first time: [link]"

## Build a Portfolio-Testimonial Connection

For each major event type you shoot, pair portfolio images with the testimonial from that client. An event coordinator viewing your website sees a gallery of great corporate shots paired with testimonials from event coordinators — perfect context matching.
