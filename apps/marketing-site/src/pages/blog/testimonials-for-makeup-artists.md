Every bride searching for her wedding makeup artist has already looked at dozens of Instagram portfolios. They all look stunning. The portfolio gets you considered. The reviews get you booked.

When a bride is deciding between you and three other makeup artists with equally beautiful feeds, your reviews are often the deciding factor.

## Who Is Booking Makeup Artists

- Brides for wedding day looks (+ bridesmaids, mothers, flower girls)
- People attending galas, proms, and formal events
- Photo and video shoots (editorial, headshots, campaigns)
- Actors and performers for stage, film, and TV
- Individuals learning to recreate specific looks (lessons)
- Theatrical and SFX clients

## When to Ask for a Review

**For bridal clients — day after the wedding:**
> "It was such a privilege to be part of your day. I hope everything was as perfect as you imagined. If you have a minute this week, I'd love a quick Google review — it means so much to brides who are trying to choose: [link]"

**For event clients — day after the event:**
> "Hope you had an amazing night! If your makeup held up and you loved how you looked, I'd be so grateful for a review: [link]"

**For lesson clients — right after:**
> "Thanks for spending the time! If you feel like you got what you came for, a review helps other people find their way here: [link]"

## The Bridal Review That Books You

*"I've been following [name] on Instagram for two years before I booked her for my wedding. She did not disappoint. The trial was collaborative — she listened to every reference image I brought and offered suggestions that I hadn't thought of. On the wedding day, she arrived early, worked efficiently, and kept everyone calm. My makeup lasted through 6 hours of photos, ceremony, and dancing. I've gotten more compliments on my wedding makeup than anything else. Book her."*

Strong elements: pre-existing trust, trial experience, collaboration quality, day-of professionalism, longevity result, and a direct endorsement.

## UGC and Photo Reviews

For makeup artists, visual testimonials are invaluable:
- Ask clients to tag you in their wedding or event photos
- Screenshot those posts and reshare as UGC (with permission)
- Create a highlights folder on Instagram for "client work" featuring tagged photos
- Include a review link in your photo delivery or the thank-you card you leave behind

## Where to Collect Reviews

1. **Google Business Profile** — Local discovery for "makeup artist near me"
2. **The Knot / WeddingWire / Zola** — Essential platforms for bridal MUAs
3. **Instagram** — Testimonials in captions and DMs; reshare tagged posts
4. **Yelp** — Some markets are active for beauty services
5. **Your website** — Portfolio + testimonials by service via [SocialProof](/)
