# Capterra Alternative for Collecting Testimonials: Own Your Reviews

Capterra is a software review directory — it's where B2B buyers go to research tools. If you've asked customers to leave you a Capterra review, you know the awkward reality: most small businesses aren't listed there, the review process is complicated for reviewers, and most importantly, **you don't own those reviews**.

If Capterra changes its algorithm or your listing loses visibility, those testimonials are gone — or at least, no longer visible to your customers.

---

## The Problem With Directory Reviews

Reviews on Capterra, G2, or any third-party directory have a fundamental limitation: **they're on someone else's platform.**

- Your potential customers have to navigate away from your website to read them
- Review visibility depends on the directory's ranking algorithm
- You can't display them in your own format, on your own site
- The reviewer experience is friction-heavy (create account, rate multiple categories, write detailed review)

Directories serve a purpose for software discovery. They're not a substitute for social proof on your own website.

---

## What You Actually Need: First-Party Testimonials

First-party testimonials are collected directly from your customers and displayed on your own site. You own them, control how they're displayed, and they're always visible to your visitors.
