# Best Testimonial Software for Small Businesses in 2025

If you're a small business owner who needs to collect and display customer testimonials, the number of tools out there is overwhelming. Trustpilot. Senja. Birdeye. Podium. Testimonial.to. Boast. EmbedReviews.

This guide cuts through it. We've compared the top tools honestly — including our own — so you can pick the right one for your business size, budget, and technical comfort level.

**Quick summary for time-strapped readers:** If you're a solo operator or small team that wants something simple and free to start, [SocialProof](/), [Senja](https://senja.io), or [Testimonial.to](https://testimonial.to) are the best starting points. Enterprise-priced tools like Birdeye and Podium are built for multi-location franchises, not solo operators.

### 2. Senja (Best free tier for testimonial walls)

**Best for:** Creators, consultants, freelancers who want a rich testimonial wall

**Pricing:** Free (limited) · Starter $19/month · Growth $49/month

Senja has one of the best free tiers in the space — you can create a public testimonial wall and share it without paying. The UI is polished and the import feature (pull from Twitter/X, LinkedIn, etc.) is genuinely useful.

**Why people choose it:** The "wall of love" display is visually impressive and easy to share.

**Limitations:** The free plan shows Senja branding. Embedding on your own website requires a paid plan. For a small business that wants a widget on their actual homepage, you'll be paying from day one.

---

### 3. Testimonial.to (Best for video testimonials)

**Best for:** Coaches, course creators, service businesses where video builds trust

**Pricing:** Free (1 space, basic) · Pro $59/month

Testimonial.to pioneered the concept of a shareable collection link for video + text testimonials. Their video recording interface is smooth and doesn't require the customer to install anything.

**Why people choose it:** If you want video testimonials and can afford $59/month, this is the gold standard.

**Limitations:** Expensive for the feature set at smaller scales. $59/month is a lot if you're a house cleaner with 20 clients.

### 4. Birdeye (Best for multi-location businesses)

**Best for:** Franchises, dental chains, auto dealerships with 5+ locations

**Pricing:** Starts at $299/month per location

Birdeye is the enterprise choice. If you have 10 dental locations and need centralized review management with automated SMS review requests, staff dashboards, and executive reporting — Birdeye does it.

**Why people choose it:** Complete review management platform with extensive integrations.

**Limitations:** Way too expensive and complex for a solo operator. $299+/month is a lot for a widget on your website.

### 5. Trustpilot (Best for consumer trust signals in e-commerce)

**Best for:** eCommerce brands that need third-party credibility verification

**Pricing:** Free (very limited) · Paid plans start at ~$250/month

Trustpilot is the most recognized review platform globally. The "Trustpilot star" in Google search results carries real trust weight, especially in European markets.

**Why people choose it:** Brand recognition. Shoppers trust Trustpilot stars in search results.

**Limitations:** The free plan is nearly useless for active collection. Paid plans are expensive. And Trustpilot has faced repeated controversies about fake reviews on the platform.

### 6. Podium (Best for local service businesses with high revenue)

**Best for:** Auto dealers, dentists, home services with large ticket sizes

**Pricing:** Starts at $399/month

Podium combines review requests with messaging — you text customers, they respond, and you can request reviews mid-conversation. It's a powerful system if your average sale is $500+.

**Why people choose it:** The combination of messaging + reviews in one platform with SMS is unique.

**Limitations:** $399/month makes no sense for a business making less than ~$20k/month. Most small businesses get 80% of the value from a $9/month testimonial tool.

👉 [Read our full Podium comparison →](/blog/podium-alternative-small-business/)

### 7. Judge.me (Best for Shopify stores)

**Best for:** Shopify store owners who want product reviews

**Pricing:** Free · Awesome plan at $15/month

Judge.me is the best-value Shopify review app. The free plan includes automatic review requests, review widgets, photo reviews, and SEO-friendly review schema. The paid plan adds features like video reviews and loyalty integrations.

**Why people choose it:** Best price-to-feature ratio for Shopify product reviews specifically.

**Limitations:** It's built for product reviews, not service testimonials. Doesn't work outside Shopify.

👉 [Read our full Judge.me comparison →](/blog/judgeme-alternative-small-business/)

### 8. EmbedSocial (Best for aggregating social media reviews)

**Best for:** Businesses that already have strong Google/Facebook/Yelp reviews and want to display them

**Pricing:** Starts at $29/month

EmbedSocial pulls reviews from Google, Facebook, Yelp, and other sources and displays them in a widget on your site. You're not collecting new testimonials — you're surfacing existing ones.

**Why people choose it:** If you have 200 Google reviews, this makes them visible on your website automatically.

**Limitations:** You're dependent on third-party review sources you don't control. Pricier than pure testimonial tools.

## How to Choose the Right Tool

Here's a simple decision framework:

**You're a solo operator, freelancer, or service business with a small budget:**
→ Start with **SocialProof** (free) or **Senja** (free tier). Both get you live in minutes.

**You want video testimonials:**
→ **Testimonial.to** (premium) or **SocialProof Pro** (affordable).

**You run a Shopify store:**
→ **Judge.me** for product reviews. **SocialProof** if you also want general service testimonials.

**You have lots of Google reviews and want to display them:**
→ **EmbedSocial** or **Trustindex** will pull from Google automatically.

**You run a multi-location business with $50k+/month revenue:**
→ **Birdeye** or **Podium** make sense at scale.

**You need enterprise credibility signals:**
→ **Trustpilot** (expensive but recognized).
