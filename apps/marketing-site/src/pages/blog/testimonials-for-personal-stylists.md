Personal styling is hard to sell in the abstract. "I'll help you look better" could mean anything. But "I hadn't worn half my wardrobe in three years and now I actually enjoy getting dressed" — that's a transformation someone can picture themselves having.

Testimonials make the invisible (confidence, clarity, ease) visible.

## Who Is Your Client

- Professionals wanting a wardrobe that reflects a career transition or promotion
- New parents who've lost their style identity postpartum
- People going through major life changes (divorce, relocation, new city)
- Executives who want to show up with more presence
- People who hate shopping and want the decision fatigue removed
- Brides building a wedding-week wardrobe beyond the dress

## When to Ask

**After the first full session or wardrobe audit:**
> "You walked in not knowing where to start and left with a clear picture. That shift is exactly what other clients need to hear about. Would you share a few sentences? [link]"

**After wearing a new outfit to a big event:**
> "How did the [event] go? If you got compliments, I'd love to know — and a quick review would help me reach other people who need this: [link]"

## What a Strong Personal Stylist Testimonial Looks Like

*"I was promoted to a leadership role and my wardrobe still looked like I was entry-level. [Stylist name] did a closet edit, helped me understand my body proportions for the first time, and built me a capsule wardrobe I actually wear. I've gotten compliments in every single meeting since. The ROI isn't just the wardrobe — it's the confidence. I recommend her to everyone who's going through a career shift."*

Strong elements: specific triggering event (promotion), the gap identified (entry-level clothes vs leadership role), process described (closet edit, body proportions, capsule), outcome (compliments, confidence), and specific referral context (career shifts).

## What Makes Styling Testimonials Different

The best styling testimonials are about confidence and internal change, not just clothes. Prompt clients to describe:
- How they felt before vs. after
- What they can do now that felt hard before (getting dressed quickly, feeling confident in meetings)
- Specific moments where the styling paid off (job interview, date, keynote)
