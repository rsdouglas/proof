# Typeform Alternative for Collecting Testimonials: Stop Using Forms, Start Getting Stories

Typeform is one of the most beautiful form builders on the internet. If you need to run a survey, collect NPS scores, or build a quiz — it's excellent.

But a lot of small business owners use Typeform to collect testimonials. And while it works, it's the wrong tool for the job. Here's why.

---

## What Typeform Gets Right

Typeform is genuinely great at:

- **Beautiful, conversational forms** — the one-question-at-a-time format feels personal
- **Survey logic** — show/hide questions based on responses
- **Data collection** — export responses to CSV, connect to Zapier
- **NPS scores** — the rating scale is clean and widely understood

If your goal is data collection, Typeform excels.

---

## Where Typeform Falls Short for Testimonials

### Responses Stay Locked in Typeform

When someone fills out your Typeform testimonial request, the response lives in Typeform's dashboard. You have to manually copy it into your website, design system, or social media.

There's no way to display those testimonials on your website automatically.

### Typeform Is Built for Data, Not Stories

A testimonial isn't a survey response. It's a piece of social proof designed to be seen — beautifully displayed, attributed, embedded on your sales pages. Typeform collects text. It doesn't publish testimonials.

### The Free Plan Is Severely Limited

Typeform's free plan caps you at 10 responses per month. If you're running a small business with 20+ customers, you'll hit that wall fast. Their paid plans start at $25/month.

### No Testimonial Display Widget

Typeform has no way to display your testimonials on your website. After collecting them, you're on your own to format and publish.
