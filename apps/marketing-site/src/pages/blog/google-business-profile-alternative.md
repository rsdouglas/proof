# Beyond Google Business Profile: Collect Testimonials You Actually Control

Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the default social proof strategy for most local businesses. Get clients to leave a Google review, and the star ratings appear in search results. Simple enough.

But for service businesses, Google reviews have real limitations. And for many service providers, they're simply not the right tool.

---

## The Problem with Google-Only Social Proof

**You don't control it.** Google can remove reviews for policy violations, merge or suppress listings, or change how profiles display at any time.

**Location matters more than you.** Google Business Profile is designed for local, location-based businesses. If your clients are national or international, or you work remotely, Google reviews are less relevant.

**Reviews lack depth.** Google reviews are 1–5 stars with optional text. For a service business, you need the story — what the client's situation was, what changed, what specific results they got.

**Not everyone will leave one.** Asking a corporate client to leave a Google review feels odd. A direct testimonial request feels personal and natural.
