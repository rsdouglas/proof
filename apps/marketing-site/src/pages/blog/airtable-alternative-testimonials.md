# Airtable Alternative for Managing Testimonials: From Database to Live Website

Airtable is a beloved tool. Half spreadsheet, half database, it's endlessly flexible and genuinely powerful. Marketing teams, agencies, and organized solopreneurs use it for everything — including tracking customer testimonials.

If you've built an Airtable base to manage your testimonials, you've got good instincts. You're thinking about testimonials systematically. The problem is that Airtable stops at organization.

---

## The Airtable Testimonials Workflow

A typical Airtable testimonial setup looks like:
- A base with columns: Customer Name, Company, Testimonial Text, Date, Approved (checkbox), Notes
- An intake form that customers fill out (or you paste responses into manually)
- A view filtered to show only approved testimonials
- ... and then nothing. Airtable can't push those to your website.

Some people build elaborate Zapier/Make automations to get Airtable data onto their websites. It works — but it's a significant setup investment, and it breaks.

---

## What Airtable Can't Do for Testimonials

### No Testimonial Widget

Airtable has no embeddable testimonial display widget. You can share a read-only Airtable view (which looks like a spreadsheet, not social proof), but you can't embed a beautiful testimonial carousel.

### No Collection Flow

Airtable doesn't have a purpose-built testimonial collection experience. Airtable forms work, but they're generic — no guidance for customers on what to write, no video recording, no brand-specific flow.

### No Auto-Sync to Website

Getting approved Airtable rows to appear as live testimonials on your website requires a custom integration. Most small business owners won't build this.
