# How Bookkeepers Get Client Testimonials That Attract Better Clients

Bookkeeping is a high-trust service. Small business owners are handing you access to their financial data, their bank accounts, and their peace of mind. Before they hire you, they want to know: *Is this person reliable? Have they helped businesses like mine?*

Testimonials answer those questions directly. And yet most bookkeepers have zero testimonials displayed anywhere.

Here's how to fix that.

## Why Bookkeeper Testimonials Work

Your potential clients are often stressed about their finances — behind on reconciliations, dreading tax season, unsure if their numbers are right. A testimonial that says "I was a mess before I hired her — now I always know where I stand financially" speaks directly to that anxiety.

The testimonial isn't about accounting features. It's about relief.

## When to Ask

**After tax season** — your client just survived tax prep without stress because of you. Ask now.

**After cleaning up a backlog** — if a client came to you with 6 months of un-reconciled books and you fixed it, ask when the work is done.

**At the 6-month mark** — client has experienced the full rhythm of working with you.

**When a client says "I don't know what I'd do without you"** — that's your cue. Ask immediately.

## Scripts for Bookkeepers

**Post-tax season email:**
> Subject: How did tax season feel this year?
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Now that tax season is in the rearview mirror, I'd love to hear how you felt about this year vs. before. If you're willing to share a few words about working together, it genuinely helps me find more great clients like you. Here's a quick link: [link]
>
> Thank you,
> [Your name]

**After a cleanup project:**
> "I'm so glad your books are caught up — that must be a relief! Would you be willing to leave a short testimonial about the process? It would mean a lot. [link]"

## What a Great Bookkeeping Testimonial Looks Like

**Weak:** "Great bookkeeper, very accurate."

**Strong:** "I was 8 months behind on my books and dreading my accountant's call. [Bookkeeper] came in, caught up everything in two weeks, found $3,400 I'd miscategorized as expenses, and set up a system so it never happens again. Tax season this year was the first one in 5 years where I wasn't panicking. Worth every penny."

That's specific, it shows problem + solution + dollar impact, and it speaks to the emotional experience. That's what books new clients.

## Confidentiality

Some clients are sensitive about their financial situation being mentioned publicly. Offer options:

- Anonymous: "Small business owner, restaurant industry"
- No financial specifics: "She caught errors I didn't even know existed"
- First name + business type only

## Where to Display Bookkeeping Testimonials

- **Your website homepage** — lead with the relief/peace of mind angle
- **Google Business Profile** — critical for local search ("bookkeeper near me")
- **LinkedIn** — recommendations from named business owners
- **Proposal decks** — include 2-3 testimonials from similar industries
