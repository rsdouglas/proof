# Yelp Alternative for Small Business: Why Owners Are Moving Beyond Yelp Reviews

Yelp built its reputation as the go-to review platform for local businesses. Restaurants, salons, contractors, dentists — if you have a local storefront, Yelp is part of the landscape.

But if you've been on Yelp for more than a year, you've probably experienced some friction.

---

## The Problem With Yelp for Small Business

### Yelp Filters Real Reviews

Yelp's "recommendation software" removes reviews it considers unreliable. In practice, this often filters out legitimate five-star reviews from real customers — especially newer Yelp accounts or people who only reviewed once.

You can ask 20 customers for a review. Yelp may show 6 of them.

### You Don't Own Your Reviews

Every review on Yelp belongs to Yelp. You can't export them, can't embed them on your website with full control, and can't bring them with you if Yelp changes its algorithm or policies.

### Yelp Advertising is Expensive — and Pushy

Yelp's sales team is aggressive. Their advertising packages run $300–$1,000+/month for most local businesses. If you don't advertise, your competitor's ads may appear on your Yelp listing.

### Yelp's Relevance Varies By Category

For restaurants: still important. For many other service businesses — attorneys, coaches, consultants, online services — Yelp drives minimal traffic. Google searches, not Yelp searches, is where customers start.
