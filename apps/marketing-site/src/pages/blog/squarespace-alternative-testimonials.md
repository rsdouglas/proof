# Squarespace Testimonials: Why the Built-In Block Falls Short (and What to Use Instead)"

Squarespace is one of the most beautiful website builders available. It's the go-to for photographers, coaches, consultants, and creative professionals who want a stunning site without coding. 

But Squarespace's built-in testimonials block has real limitations that frustrate growing businesses. Here's an honest breakdown — and a better path forward.

---

## What Squarespace's Testimonials Block Does

Squarespace includes a Quote block and a Testimonials block that lets you manually add text quotes. You type them in, they display on your page. Simple, clean, in your site's design.

**Works fine if:**
- You have 3–5 testimonials and won't need to update them often
- You only want text, no video
- You don't want a way for customers to submit testimonials directly

---

## Where It Falls Short

### No Collection Mechanism

Squarespace has no built-in way to ask customers for testimonials. You have to collect them yourself (email, copy-paste, hope they respond) and then manually add them to your site. As you grow, this becomes a real time drain.

### No Video

Video testimonials convert significantly better than text. Squarespace's testimonials block is text-only. Adding video requires workarounds (YouTube embeds, etc.) that look cobbled together.

### Manual Updates Are a Drag

Every time you want to add a new testimonial, you have to go into the Squarespace editor, find the block, add the quote, re-check design, and publish. For active businesses, this means testimonials stay stale.

### Limited Display Formats

The Squarespace testimonials block has a limited number of layout options. You can't do a grid of cards, a scrolling carousel with photos, or a wall of love without custom code.
