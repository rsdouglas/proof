When someone finds a stylist they trust, they stay for years. But getting that first appointment from a stranger who doesn't know your work yet — that's the hard part. That's where reviews do the work.

## Who Is Looking for a Hair Stylist

- New clients moving to a new city and starting from scratch
- Clients who've had a bad experience and are looking for someone better
- Clients seeking a specific specialty: color, keratin, natural/curly, extensions, balayage
- Clients with challenging hair types looking for expertise
- Brides looking for wedding hair services

## When to Ask

**Immediately after the appointment, with the before/after photo:**
> "Love how it turned out! If you're happy, a quick Google review while you're still excited about it would mean so much: [link]"

**After a dramatic transformation:**
> "This was a big change and you look amazing. If you're loving it, sharing your experience helps other clients who are nervous about making the same leap find the courage: [link]"

## The Before/After Combo

Ask for permission to post before/after photos. When a client loves the result and you post the photo, they often share it. Combine the post with a review request. Many clients are delighted to be featured.

## What a Strong Hair Stylist Review Looks Like

*"I have naturally curly hair and most stylists either flat-iron everything or cut it dry without understanding the curl pattern. [Name] asked about my routine, explained what she was going to do and why, and gave me the best haircut I've had in years. The curls actually fell exactly right. I've already booked my next appointment and sent two friends. Stop looking — this is your stylist."*

Strong elements: specific hair challenge, explanation of technique, validation (best in years), specific outcome, repeat booking, and direct recommendation.
