Choosing a midwife is one of the most trust-intensive decisions a family makes. Whether a couple is planning a home birth, a birth center birth, or seeking a CNM in a hospital setting, they are selecting someone to care for them in their most vulnerable moments.

Testimonials from previous clients are your most powerful trust-building tool. Birth stories told by families you've cared for will reach other families who need to hear exactly those words.

## Who Is Looking

- Couples planning home births
- Families seeking birth center care as an alternative to hospital delivery
- Hospital patients who want midwifery care within an OB/hospital setting
- Parents of subsequent pregnancies who want a different birth experience
- Families seeking culturally affirming or holistic maternity care

## HIPAA and Privacy

Like all healthcare providers, you must handle testimonial requests carefully:
- You may ask clients to voluntarily share their experience publicly
- You may not share or reference specific health information in your request
- For website testimonials, get written consent before publishing — especially for birth stories
- Let families choose how much of their story they share

## When to Ask

**Postpartum visit (1-2 weeks after birth):**
> "It has been such an honor to support your family through this. When you feel ready, and if your experience was something you'd want other families to know about, I'd be so grateful if you shared your story: [link]"

**At the 6-week visit:**
> "As we close out your postpartum care, I'd love for you to share your birth experience — it's exactly what other families searching for support need to hear: [link]"

## What a Strong Midwifery Testimonial Looks Like

*"After a difficult first birth in a hospital, we knew we wanted something different. [Midwife's name] was present for every prenatal appointment in a way no OB had ever been — she knew our story, our fears, and our goals without us having to repeat ourselves every time. When labor began, she arrived within the hour and stayed with us through the entire birth. Our daughter was born in the water at home, and the entire experience was peaceful and empowering. We are forever grateful."*

Strong elements: contrast with previous experience, continuity of care, presence during labor, emotional outcome, gratitude.
