When someone needs a local service — a plumber, a locksmith, a painter, a landscaper — they don't browse company websites for hours. They check reviews, ask a neighbor, or click the first result with strong ratings.

Testimonials and reviews ARE your local marketing strategy. Nothing else comes close.

## Why Reviews Matter More for Local Service

- **You're invisible without them.** Google ranks local businesses partly on review count and recency.
- **Trust has to be established fast.** A stranger calling to let someone into their home or car needs social proof quickly.
- **You compete with national chains** that have marketing budgets — your authentic local reviews are the differentiator.
- **Word of mouth is local by nature.** A testimonial on your site amplifies what your best customers are already saying privately.

## Who Is Your Customer

Local service customers are searching with urgency:
- "emergency plumber near me"
- "best house painter [city]"
- "affordable landscaping [neighborhood]"
- "licensed electrician [zip code]"

They're looking for speed, reliability, fair pricing, and professionalism. Your testimonials need to address exactly those concerns.

## When to Ask

**Immediately after service completion:**
> "Glad we could help! If you're happy with the work, a quick Google review keeps my business running — took less than 30 seconds last time a customer did it: [link]"

**Via SMS the same evening:**
> "This is [name] — you're all set! If you'd give us a quick review it'd mean a lot: [link] Takes one minute."

## What a Strong Local Service Testimonial Looks Like

*"[Company] came out same-day when my pipe burst on a Sunday. The technician was professional, explained what was broken and why, and charged exactly what they quoted over the phone — no surprises. I've used them twice now and referred them to my neighbor. For any plumbing in [city], this is the only call you need to make."*

Strong elements: same-day responsiveness (urgency), Sunday availability (inconvenient situations), transparency in pricing (no surprises), repeat business, referral, and geographic specificity.
