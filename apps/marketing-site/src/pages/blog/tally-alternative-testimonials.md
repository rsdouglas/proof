# Tally Alternative for Testimonials: Why a Form Tool Falls Short

Tally is an excellent free form builder. If you need to build a survey, collect event registrations, or run a quick quiz — Tally is genuinely impressive, especially given that it's free.

It's also one of the most common tools small business owners reach for when they want to start collecting testimonials: "I'll just make a Tally form."

That works. Until you realize what's missing.

---

## What a Tally Testimonial Workflow Looks Like

1. Build a Tally form with fields for name, title/company, testimonial text
2. Share the link with clients
3. Responses come in as form submissions
4. Export to CSV or view in Tally dashboard
5. Copy-paste testimonials manually into your website

It gets the job done. The problem is everything that comes after collection: display, management, and iteration.

---

## Where Tally Falls Short for Testimonials

### No Display Widget

Tally collects data. It doesn't display it. You get form responses in a spreadsheet. Getting those onto your website as a clean, styled testimonial carousel or grid requires building that yourself — either with custom code or copying and pasting into your site builder.

### Ongoing Management

As you collect more testimonials, you want to be able to approve, hide, and feature them. Tally gives you a spreadsheet. That's fine for 5 testimonials; it breaks down at 50.

### No Social Proof Display

Tally won't inject a "47 happy clients" counter into your header, or automatically rotate your best testimonials on your services page. That's not what it's for.
