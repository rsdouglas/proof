In ecommerce, a product with 50 reviews converts at dramatically higher rates than the same product with 0 reviews — even if the 50-review product is priced higher.

Customer reviews are not a nice-to-have. They are a revenue lever.

## Why Reviews Matter More in Ecommerce

Unlike a local service where customers can read faces and tour a physical space, online shoppers have nothing but photos, descriptions, and other people's words. They're asking: *Does this actually work? Is the quality real? Will it show up on time?*

Reviews answer every one of those questions.

## The Post-Purchase Review Sequence

**Email 1 (Day 7 after delivery):**
> Subject: How did your [product name] arrive?
> "Hi [Name] — your order should have arrived by now. We'd love to know how it looks in person! [Leave a review → link]"

**Email 2 (Day 21 — for experience/consumable products):**
> Subject: How's the [product] treating you?
> "You've had your [product] for a few weeks now. If you're loving it, we'd be so grateful for a review — it helps other shoppers make confident decisions: [link]"

**SMS (optional, if opted in):**
> "Your [brand] order arrived! Happy with it? Drop us a quick review: [link] — takes 60 seconds."

## What a Strong Ecommerce Review Looks Like

*"I was skeptical because I've ordered similar products from three other brands and been disappointed. This one is genuinely different — the quality is immediately obvious when you open the box. It does exactly what it says it does and has held up after 2 months of daily use. Finally found my go-to."*

Strong elements: comparison to competitors, initial skepticism overcome, quality observation, durability, and a verdict.

## Review Display Strategy

Where to show reviews on your site:
- **Product page** — Below the fold with star rating aggregation above the fold
- **Homepage** — Brand testimonials from diverse customers
- **Cart/checkout** — Reassurance at the highest-anxiety moment
- **Email flows** — Include a recent review in abandoned cart and browse-abandonment emails

Use [SocialProof](/) to pull live reviews into any page of your site without rebuilding your tech stack.

## Photo and Video Reviews

User-generated photos and videos are gold:
- More authentic than product photos
- Show scale, fit, and real-world context
- Generate social content for Instagram and TikTok

Incentivize with: "Send us a photo with your review for 10% off your next order."

## Where to Collect Reviews

1. **Your own site** — Via [SocialProof](/) or Shopify review apps
2. **Google Shopping** — Reviews feed Google's product listing stars
3. **Trustpilot / ResellerRatings** — Third-party credibility for brand-level trust
4. **Amazon** — If you sell there, a review engine is non-negotiable
5. **Social media** — DMs, comments, and UGC that you can reshare with permission
