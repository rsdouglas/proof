# Using Notion to Collect Testimonials? Here's a Better Way

A surprising number of solopreneurs and freelancers use Notion to collect testimonials. The workflow usually looks something like this: create a Notion form, share the link with clients, manually copy responses into a database, then copy-paste quotes onto your website whenever you update it.

It works. Kind of. But it's creating work you don't need to do.

---

## The Notion Testimonials Workflow (and Where It Breaks Down)

**Step 1: Create a Notion form or page where clients can leave feedback**  
This involves either Notion Forms (if you have access) or a shared Notion page where clients type their feedback.

**Step 2: Manually review and organize responses**  
You get an email or check your Notion database periodically.

**Step 3: Copy the good ones to your website**  
This means opening your website builder, finding the testimonials section, pasting the quote, formatting it, and republishing.

**Where it breaks down:**
- Step 3 almost never happens as often as Step 1 generates testimonials
- Clients can see each other's responses if you're not careful with permissions
- No video — Notion handles text and photos, not recorded video testimonials
- The workflow depends entirely on you manually doing the copy-paste loop

---

## What Notion Is Great At

Notion is phenomenal for personal knowledge management, team wikis, project tracking, and content planning. If you use Notion for your business, keep using it for what it does well.

It's just not built to be a testimonial collection and display platform.
