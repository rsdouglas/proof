Insurance is the hardest thing to sell. You're asking someone to pay for something they hope they never need.

Clients don't just buy a policy — they buy trust in the person who sold it to them. When a claim happens, they need to know you'll be there. Testimonials are how you prove that before the first call ever happens.

## The Trust Problem in Insurance

A homeowner searching for a new insurance agent sees dozens of options, most with slick websites and identical promises: "We find you the best coverage at the best price."

Without testimonials, you're indistinguishable from every competitor. With the right testimonials, you're the obvious choice.

The key is specificity. Generic testimonials don't move the needle:
- ❌ "Great agent, very helpful."
- ✅ "When our basement flooded, Amy called our adjuster that same afternoon. We had a check in 12 days. I tell everyone in our neighborhood — she's the one to call."

Claim experiences, life events, and specific numbers are gold.

## What Insurance Testimonials Should Cover

### 1. Claims experiences
> "I filed a claim after my car was totaled. Marcus walked me through every step, fought for a fair payout, and we settled in two weeks. I didn't have to do anything except answer a few calls."

This is the #1 fear clients have. Address it directly.

### 2. Explaining coverage
> "I had no idea what I actually had until I sat down with her. She rewrote my home and auto bundle, got me better coverage, and saved me $400 a year. I wish I'd done this sooner."

Demonstrates expertise and results.

### 3. Life transitions
> "When we bought our first house, we were overwhelmed. He explained everything in plain English and never made us feel dumb for asking basic questions. Still our agent five years later."

### 4. Long-term relationships
> "I've been with David for 11 years. Every time something changes — new car, new kid, business insurance — he updates everything proactively. You don't have to remember anything."

Retention and loyalty signals are powerful for other clients considering switching.

## When and How to Ask

**Best moments to ask for testimonials:**
- After a successful claim resolution
- After a policy review when you've saved them money
- After helping them through a major life event (new home, new baby, retirement)
- At the annual review, if they express satisfaction

**Ask in person first:**
> "I'm really glad that worked out for you. Would you be willing to share your experience? I'm building up reviews on my website and Google — it really helps families like yours find me."

**Follow-up email:**

Subject: Your feedback means the world to our clients

Hi [Name],

Thank you for trusting me with your coverage — it's been a pleasure working with you.

A quick favor: would you share a few sentences about your experience? Your words help other families in [city] find an agent they can trust.

[Leave a testimonial →]

Takes 2 minutes. Means everything to us.

[Your name]

## Where Testimonials Move the Needle for Insurance

### Website homepage
A rotating testimonial widget with real client names, photos, and stories signals you're a real person people trust — not just a policy vending machine.

### Agent profile on aggregator sites
Sites like Insurify, Policygenius, and local directories let agents add testimonials. Use them.

### Claims handling page
If you have a "what to do if you have a claim" page, add testimonials from clients who had claims. This is where anxiety peaks — reassurance there is worth gold.

### "Why work with me" page
If you have an about page or a page explaining your value versus going direct, testimonials from long-term clients (5+ years) are your strongest argument.

### Email signature
A short quote and a link to your reviews page works quietly but consistently.
