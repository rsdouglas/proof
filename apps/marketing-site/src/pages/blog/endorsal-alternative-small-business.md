If you've been looking at Endorsal for collecting customer testimonials, you've probably noticed it's a solid product — but one that's built with agencies and larger teams in mind. For a small business owner who just wants to collect testimonials and put them on their website without a steep learning curve, there's a simpler option.

## What Is Endorsal?

Endorsal is a testimonial collection platform that lets you request and display testimonials. It includes collection forms, embeddable widgets, and some automation features. It's been around since 2018 and has a loyal user base — mostly freelancers and agencies.

## Where Endorsal Falls Short for Small Businesses

### Pricing that scales against you
Endorsal's free plan is extremely limited. To get useful features — like importing existing testimonials or removing Endorsal branding — you're quickly looking at paid tiers. For a solopreneur or small business owner, paying monthly for a testimonials tool before you've seen ROI is a hard sell.

### Interface designed for power users
Endorsal has a lot of features. That's great if you're an agency managing multiple clients. It's overwhelming if you're a salon owner who just wants to add a testimonial widget to your Squarespace site.

### Branding on free plan
The free plan shows "Powered by Endorsal" on your widget. For businesses trying to look polished and professional, this is a problem.
