Looking for a Senja Alternative? Here's Why Small Business Owners Switch to SocialProof

Senja is well-designed testimonial software. If you've used it, you know it works. The collection forms are clean, the widgets look good, and the "wall of love" feature is genuinely nice.

But at $29/month — which is where the useful features live — it's priced for agencies managing 10+ client accounts. If you're a solo business owner who wants to add a handful of testimonials to your website, you're paying $350/year for software built for someone doing 10x your scale.

That's why small business owners end up looking for alternatives.

What Senja Is Great At

Let's be honest before we get into why you might want something different.

Senja is genuinely good at:

  • Video testimonials — if you want customers to record a short video review, Senja has the best UX for this
  • Agency workflows — managing multiple client accounts, white-labeling, team access
  • "Wall of Love" pages — a dedicated /testimonials page that pulls everything in automatically
  • Integrations — Slack notifications, Zapier hooks, the full suite

If you need any of those things, Senja is worth considering.

Where Senja Falls Short for Small Businesses

Price

Senja's free plan is quite limited — you get 15 testimonials total, ever. Once you hit that, you're on the $29/month tier.

For a freelancer, coach, or small Shopify store owner who just wants to display 10-15 customer quotes on their homepage, that's an expensive proposition.

Complexity

Senja has a lot of features. That's great if you need them. If you're just trying to collect a few testimonials and put them on your website, the interface can feel overwhelming for what's actually a simple task.

Built for agencies, not solo owners

This isn't a criticism — it's just how the product is positioned. Senja's workflows assume you're managing multiple "spaces" (their term for separate accounts). For a solo business owner with one website, that mental model adds friction.

Other Senja Alternatives Worth Knowing

While you're comparing, here's the broader landscape:

Testimonial.to: Similar to Senja, also supports video, priced at $25-50/month. Excellent product, but same pricing problem for small businesses.

Elfsight Testimonials: Cheap ($5/month) and pulls from Google Reviews or lets you add testimonials manually. Limitation: slow load times, shows Elfsight branding on free plan, no collection mechanism.

DIY with a CMS: Some businesses just add a testimonials section in Webflow or Squarespace manually. Works fine if you don't need a collection form — you just copy-paste what customers say. The downside is there's no approval workflow and updating it requires going back into your CMS every time.
