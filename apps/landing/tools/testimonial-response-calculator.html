What response rate should you expect for testimonial requests?

Response rates vary significantly depending on your relationship with the customer and how you reach out. Here's what the data shows:

Cold email outreach: 3–8%

If you're reaching people who don't know you well, expect a low response rate. Subject lines matter enormously here — personalization can double your rate.

Warm email to past customers: 10–20%

The most common scenario for small businesses. You've served them well; they're happy. A well-timed, personal email asking for a testimonial typically gets a 10–20% response rate.

Post-purchase / in-person ask: 25–45%

Asking right after a great experience — in person, or via a receipt email — is the highest-converting approach. The customer's satisfaction is fresh, making them more likely to respond.

SMS / text message: 20–35%

Texts have much higher open rates than email (98% vs. 20%). Even a simple "Hi [Name], can you leave us a quick review?" via text often outperforms a lengthy email.

How to improve your testimonial response rate

1. Time it right

Ask within 48–72 hours of a completed project or purchase. The customer's satisfaction is highest then. Waiting weeks causes the memory to fade.

2. Make it effortless

Give them a direct link to a simple form. Don't make them write a long essay — one or two sentences is enough. The less friction, the higher your response rate.

3. Personalize every message

Use their name and reference the specific product or service they purchased. A generic "please review us" gets ignored. A personal note gets responses.

4. Send a single follow-up

Send a brief reminder 5–7 days later for non-responders. One follow-up can increase responses by 40%. Don't send more than one — it becomes spam.

5. Show what you're asking for

Include an example testimonial in your email so customers know what "good" looks like. This reduces the blank-page anxiety that kills response rates.

How many testimonials do you actually need?

  • 5–10 testimonials — Enough to get started. Cover your homepage and product page.
  • 15–25 testimonials — A healthy library. Rotate them on your site and use the best ones in ads.
  • 50+ testimonials — A strong social proof moat. Display counts ("Join 50+ happy customers") and create category-specific pages.

Quality matters more than quantity. Two detailed, specific testimonials from real customers beat ten generic "great service!" reviews every time.
