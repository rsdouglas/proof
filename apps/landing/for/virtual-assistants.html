Social Proof for Virtual Assistants

As a virtual assistant, you're asking clients to trust you with their calendar, their inbox, and sometimes their most sensitive business operations. They can't shake your hand or see your office. Your website — specifically the testimonials on it — is the first and often only impression you make. Make it count.

Why VAs win or lose on reputation

The VA market is crowded. From Upwork to direct outreach, a client has dozens of options. What separates VAs who command premium rates from those racing to the bottom is demonstrated trust. Testimonials from past clients who say exactly what you helped them accomplish — and how reliable you were — do that work automatically.

One strong testimonial from a client saying "Jennifer saved me 15 hours a week and my business runs smoother than ever" is worth more than a perfectly formatted portfolio.

The testimonials that win VA retainer clients

  • Time savings: "Reclaimed 20 hours a week. She handles my email, scheduling, and research — I just make decisions."
  • Reliability: "Always responsive, always delivers before deadlines. I've never had to follow up."
  • Initiative: "Doesn't just do what she's asked — proactively spots problems and fixes them."
  • Specialty skills: "Handled my entire email marketing setup — Mailchimp, automations, the whole thing."
  • Communication: "Weekly updates, clear on capacity, and always honest about what's on her plate."
