Why gym owners struggle with testimonials

😬

Asking feels awkward

Pulling a member aside mid-workout to ask for a review is uncomfortable for both of you.

⭐

Google reviews go to Google

When members leave reviews, they go to Google — not to the page where your next prospect is deciding to buy.

📸

Before/after is complicated

Not every member wants photos shared. A written testimonial respects privacy and still converts visitors.

🔧

Tech is a barrier

Most gym owners don't have time to learn a new platform. You need something that works in minutes.

How SocialProof works for gyms

  1. Sign up and get your collection link

    Takes 2 minutes. No credit card required. Your link is ready immediately — share it with members via text, email, or a QR code at the front desk.

  2. Members type their testimonial — no app download

    They click the link, type what they'd like to say, and submit. Works on any phone. Takes under a minute.

  3. You approve and display it

    Review every testimonial before it goes live. Approve the good ones, skip anything vague. Your website only shows what you choose.

  4. Paste the widget on your gym's website

    Copy one line of code. Works on Squarespace, Wix, WordPress, Webflow, or any site with an HTML embed option.

When to ask for a testimonial

🎯 Best moments to send your collection link

  • After a member hits a milestone — first 5kg lost, first pull-up, first 5K
  • At the end of a challenge or 6-week program
  • After a member renews their membership or upgrades
  • When a member thanks you in person — "Mind if I send you a link to write that down?"
  • 30 days into a new membership, once they've seen real progress
"I'd tried three other gyms in the area and never stuck with it. The coaching here is different — trainers actually check in on you. I've lost 12kg in four months and I look forward to coming in." — Sarah K., member since 2024

That kind of testimonial converts new membership inquiries. It's specific, it's credible, and it addresses the exact objection ("will I actually stick with it?") that holds people back.
