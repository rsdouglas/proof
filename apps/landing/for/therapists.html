Testimonials for therapy: a sensitive subject, handled carefully

We know the concern: collecting testimonials from therapy clients raises questions about privacy, boundaries, and professional ethics. These are real considerations — and they're why SocialProof's approach works for therapists specifically.

Clients submit only what they want to share. Your collection link asks for a written testimonial at whatever level of detail the client is comfortable with. They control what they say — you don't prompt them toward specifics about their issues or diagnosis.

You approve everything before it goes public. Nothing appears on your site without your explicit approval. If a testimonial contains information you think is too personal or specific, you simply don't approve it.

Clients can share broadly or keep it vague. "Working with [therapist name] changed my relationship with anxiety" is a powerful testimonial that doesn't reveal anything sensitive. Your clients know this — they'll self-censor appropriately.

Note: Always check your licensing board's guidelines on testimonials. Some boards have restrictions; others don't. This page isn't legal or ethical advice.
