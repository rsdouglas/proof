SocialProof vs Reviews.io — honest comparison for small business

Reviews.io is a powerful platform built for enterprise review management. SocialProof is built for small businesses that want authentic testimonials on their website — without the $89/month starting price.

Try SocialProof free → See a live demo

TL;DR

Reviews.io is a full-stack review platform with integrations into Trustpilot, Google, and major ecommerce platforms. If you're a mid-market retailer managing thousands of product reviews across multiple channels, it's a serious tool. If you're a service business or solopreneur who just wants to collect a few authentic testimonials and put them on your website — it's 10x the price you need to pay and 10x the complexity.

SocialProof: best for

  • Service businesses: coaches, consultants, therapists
  • Local businesses: restaurants, salons, studios
  • Solopreneurs who want a simple trust widget
  • Anyone who needs testimonials on their site in under an hour
  • Startups and early-stage products on a tight budget
  • Non-technical founders — no developer required

Reviews.io: best for

  • Ecommerce brands with product review needs at scale
  • Businesses running Google Shopping ads (syndication matters)
  • Teams with a dedicated marketing manager
  • Companies that need Trustpilot + Google + site all synced
  • High-volume review management (100+ reviews/month)

Pick the right tool for your situation

You run a yoga studio and want 3 testimonials on your homepage

Use SocialProof. Send a collection link to your best students. Approve the responses. Paste the embed code. Done in an afternoon. $0.

You run a 500 SKU Shopify store and need product reviews

Reviews.io makes sense. You need verified purchase reviews, syndication to Google, and rich media. That's their wheelhouse.

You're a freelance designer who wants testimonials on your portfolio

Use SocialProof. It's free, takes 10 minutes to set up, and displays beautifully on any site.

You need star ratings to show up in Google Shopping results

Reviews.io handles this. Seller ratings syndication to Google is a specific feature we don't offer yet.
