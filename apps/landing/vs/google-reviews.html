Why Google Reviews alone aren't enough

Google Reviews are a trust signal for discovery. But once someone lands on your website, they need proof on the page they're reading — not a link that takes them away to Google.

❌ What you can't do with Google Reviews on your site

  • Embed a testimonials section on your homepage
  • Show 5-star quotes on a landing page or pricing page
  • Display reviews without linking visitors away to Google
  • Control which reviews appear (negative ones show too)
  • Collect feedback from customers without a Google account
  • Get rich, story-driven testimonials with photos and context

✅ What SocialProof gives you

  • A testimonial widget that lives on your website
  • A shareable link to collect testimonials anytime
  • Moderation — you approve before anything goes live
  • Custom styling that matches your brand
  • Rich format: name, role, company, photo, quote, rating
  • Works even if your customer doesn't have a Google account

🔑 Pro tip: Use Google Reviews AND SocialProof together

Google Reviews = more people find you on Google Maps and local search.
SocialProof = once they're on your site, they see proof that converts.

Many SocialProof customers ask happy clients to leave both: a Google Review for SEO, and a SocialProof testimonial for the website. Takes 5 extra minutes and doubles your social proof everywhere that matters.

Who should use what?

✅ Use SocialProof if you…

  • Want testimonials embedded on your own website
  • Sell online (course, SaaS, agency, consulting)
  • Want to approve reviews before they go live
