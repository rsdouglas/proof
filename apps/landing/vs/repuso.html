The key difference: owned vs. borrowed

Repuso is built around aggregating reviews you didn't originally collect — pulling from Google, Facebook, Yelp, and other platforms. That's useful if you already have a review presence there. But it means your testimonials live on someone else's platform, subject to their API changes, policies, and availability.

SocialProof works differently: you send customers a direct link, they write a testimonial, you approve it, it appears on your site. The testimonials are yours — not dependent on any third-party platform staying online or keeping your access active.

Who wins for small businesses?

✓ SocialProof is better if you…

  • Want to collect original testimonials directly from customers
  • Don't have an established Google/Facebook review presence yet
  • Want full ownership and control of your testimonials
  • Need a free starting point with no credit card
  • Care about schema markup for Google rich snippets

Repuso might be better if you…

  • Already have 50+ reviews on Google or Facebook
  • Want to aggregate reviews from multiple platforms in one widget
  • Primarily need to display existing third-party reviews
  • Don't need a direct testimonial collection flow
